What Makes Dave's Hot Chicken Worth Finding?

Opened in 2017 by four Armenian-American friends in a Los Angeles parking lot, Dave's Hot Chicken became a viral sensation almost overnight. After being featured on food blog Eater LA, lines stretched around the block. Now backed by celebrity investors including Drake, Samuel L. Jackson, Maria Shriver, and Michael Strahan, and acquired by private equity firm Roark Capital in a $1 billion deal in 2025, Dave's is more than a trend — it's a fast-food empire in the making.

The menu is intentionally simple: chicken tenders and sliders, fries, kale slaw, mac and cheese, and Dave's Sauce. What sets it apart is the 7 spice levels — from No Spice all the way to Reaper, which requires customers to sign a waiver. Every single location serves halal chicken, making it widely accessible.
